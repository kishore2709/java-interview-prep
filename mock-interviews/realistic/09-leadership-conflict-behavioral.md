# Realistic Mock 9 — Leadership, Conflict & Behavioral

**Format:** Client interview, pure behavioral · **Duration:** 45 min
**Use as:** interviewer script or self-practice. Use STAR
(Situation/Task/Action/Result) structure out loud, even though you won't
be asked for it by name.
**Real anchor:** the Tech Anchor role at WSI (reviews, offshore
coordination, decisions, delivery) — see [story-bank.md](story-bank.md).

---

**Ask:** "Tell me about your Tech Anchor role — or whatever the largest
team/scope you've been the senior technical voice for — how big, and what
was your actual day-to-day involvement?"
- *Cross-question:* "What changed about your own job as that team or
  scope grew — what did you stop doing personally?"

**Ask:** "Describe a real conflict with a team member or a leader above
you — what was the actual disagreement, and how did it resolve?"
- *Cross-question:* "What did the other person have right that you didn't
  initially want to admit?"

**Ask:** "Describe a technical disagreement specifically — architecture or
design — where both sides had a legitimate case."
- *Follow-up:* "How was it actually resolved — data, authority, deadline,
  compromise?"
- *Cross-question:* "If you were overruled, what did you do next? If you
  won the argument, how did you know you were actually right and not just
  more persuasive?"

**Ask:** "What's your experience working with distributed or offshore
teams? What's genuinely the hardest part, honestly?"
- *Cross-question:* "Give me one specific thing you changed about how you
  communicate because of a real failure caused by distance or timezone,
  not a generic best practice."

**Ask:** "How do you keep code quality consistent across a team that isn't
always online at the same time as you?"

**Ask:** "Share a real example where you personally introduced a bug into
production. How did you find out, and how did you fix it?"
- *Cross-question:* "What in your own process let that bug through — what
  specifically changed afterward so the same *category* of mistake is
  less likely, not just that exact bug?"

**Ask:** "What is technical debt, in your own words — and how do you keep
a lid on it without stalling delivery?"
- *Cross-question:* "Give me a real case where you knowingly shipped with
  debt anyway. What was the tradeoff, and did it bite you later?"

**Ask:** "How do you make sure an architectural decision actually serves
business goals rather than just being the technically interesting choice
to you personally?"

**Ask:** "Describe your approach to code review — what do you actually
look for, and how do you deliver hard feedback without it landing badly?"
- *Cross-question:* "Tell me about a code review where you were
  initially wrong about your own feedback. What happened?"

---

## Debrief (for self-practice)

**This round is graded on specificity and honesty, not polish.** A
slightly messy, real story beats a smooth, generic one — these
interviewers have heard the rehearsed version many times already.

**Strong signals:** every story names a real timeframe and a measurable
outcome; the production-bug story includes what you personally got wrong,
not just what you fixed; the "overruled" cross-question gets a real
answer instead of being dodged.

**Weak signals:** conflict story where you were obviously right and the
other person was obviously wrong; tech-debt answer stays abstract with no
specific tradeoff you actually made and lived with the consequences of.
